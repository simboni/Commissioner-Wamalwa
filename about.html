My Journey

A life spent defending human dignity

“As a Commissioner of Human Rights in Kenya, I have faced numerous challenges — but also incredible moments of fulfilment. Come along with me on this eye-opening journey as a human rights champion.”

Human Rights Defender Gender Specialist Mental-Health Consultant Governance & Peace Scholar
Serving Kenya since the early 2000s

Dr. Dennis Wamalwa, PhD, is a Commissioner with the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights (KNCHR). He also serves as a Board Member and Vice-Chairperson of the Intersex Persons Society of Kenya (IPSK). A governance expert, community organiser, social-justice advocate, gender specialist, and human rights defender, Dr. Wamalwa brings a wealth of experience in advocacy initiatives across Kenya and the African continent.

He is an associate research consultant with the AFRIBA Research Consortium, which contributes to social change in Africa, and a member of Honor and Diligence Support Service — an international consortium of counsellors and psychologists offering remote psychological support across Africa.

Dr. Wamalwa has more than 20 years of experience as a lecturer, trainer, researcher, and mental-health and psychosocial well-being consultant, working with international, national, and local NGOs, corporates, government, health, and religious institutions. He is a registered member of the Kenya Counsellors and Psychologists Association, holding Associate Counsellor Supervisor status.

He holds a PhD in Peace and Conflict Studies and a Master's in Counselling Studies from the University of Manchester — with 20 publications in peer-reviewed journals and 25+ postgraduate scholars supervised to completion.

See his current work
2021 – Present

Mental-Health & Psychosocial Consultant · Honor and Diligence

Offering remote psychological counselling to survivors of the Palma attack across Africa, and worldwide mental-health support and talks.

2015 – 2021

Part-time Lecturer · MMUST, Africa Nazarene & MKU

Lecturing and supervising Bachelor's and Master's students; mentoring students toward holistic mental well-being through counselling.

2020

COVID-19 National Response · Red Cross & Ministry of Health

Trained frontline health workers on Psychological First Aid and self-care; coordinated counselling across quarantine centres and supervised clinical teams.

2019

Psychosocial Counsellor & Trainer · Peace Brigades International

Supporting staff under distressful conditions, including the rehabilitation of victims of political torture, through individual and group counselling.

2017 – 2018

Consultant · IREX (Washington DC), SAFE Program Africa

Designed and delivered holistic psychosocial safety training for human rights defenders, journalists, and lawyers across East and the Horn of Africa.

2007 – 2010

Lead Psychologist · Independent Medico-Legal Unit (IMLU)

Supported torture victims of police brutality during the Mt Elgon 'Okoa Maisha' interventions; provided prison counselling and staff debriefing.

2005 – 2016

Senior Psychological Counsellor · Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF-B)

Led a multi-cultural team in emergency preparedness and response, trauma interventions, SGBV, and disaster survivor support — including the 2009 Sachangwan tragedy.

2000 – 2005

Team Leader & Counsellor · GOAL Street Program

Counselled, reintegrated, and reconciled over 250 children with their families across Kenya, with debriefing and supervision of field teams.
